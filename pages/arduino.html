Arduino

I was halfway across the Atlantic in a sailing boat, when we had a chat about digital barometers, it turns out that nothing on the market was a good fit for this boat. Too expensive, too big, too small, not enough featuresâ€¦ there was no perfect fit. Ultimately we never changed ours, but that laid the foundation of this project; build a digital barometer.

Design

The basic design brief came from the boat.

  • Small
  • Cheap
  • Low power
  • Backlit
  • history
  • Graph?

It also had to be relatively quick and easy to make, so I settled on a design based around an Arduino microcontroller, a display from an old Nokia phone, and a small barometer module. Later I added a timer module to help with the history/graph feature.

Libraries

All the different parts have been used by hobbyists before, so there were already some libraries available which helped making the software quick and easy.

  • Display - display library

Circuit layout

Putting it all together at first was on breadboard with wires and a few resistors to connect it all, then I transfered that onto a prototype shield, which fits inside a small waterproof case.
