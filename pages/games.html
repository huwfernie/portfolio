Games

Games make life a little more fun, and apart from the odd tear-your-hair-out moment they are also fun to make. So far I have kept focused on the maths heavy side of things, but that is also more of a challenge to build.

Dots and Boxes

Dots and Boxes is the game we used to play when all we had was a scrap of paper and the back of an envelope. I built this version as part of a Web Development training course in 2017. It was a great test to overcome the hurdles of making a game from scratch.

Initially I hard coded the board and added jQuery for the game logic, later I created a fuction that would build the board at game start and that let me introduce different board sizes.

For styling I tried to incorporate the idea of school-desks, pencils and scraps of paper, and this worked well on the desktop site. For mobile their was too little screen space, so that reverts to a plain display. I also put in a feature that will take your initial and let you claim a square when you finish it, just like the real thing.

Space Invaders

I had too much spare time on my hands and my JavaScript was getting a bit rusty, so I began a Space Invaders project. It was really good to have something to aim for, but it was tough tyring to keep any continuity as it was built in bursts of 5 minutes here and there.

The first task was a centered board, then a div that could move with key presses. I decided early on not to make this responsive - the original wasn't, so I didn't add any on screen buttons.

Then came a div that was being shot upwards. It had to be created when you press space key and animate upwards, then destroy itself when it reaches the top of the board. This was all done inside a loop function, that would update on a setTimeout if there were shots on screen. To make the game harder I introduced a limit of 3 shots onscreen at once.

The aliens are also divs, in an array, they don't act quite like the originals who close gaps in their ranks, and their is only one line of them, but they serve their purpose. Once I added the aliens I tried having a separate looping function similar to the shots, but I realised that syncing these two loops was too hard, so instead I moved the aliens and shots inside one loop.

To finish, I had a win or loose function that is set to run either when the aliens get to the defences, or the player defeats all the aliens. It's quite a simple game, with no scores, lives or levels; It's still fun.
